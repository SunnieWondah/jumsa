JUMSA Mentor-Mentee Initiative Forum

AUTHOR
JUMSA Press
DATE
Dec 1, 2023
CATEGORY
  • Broadcast
Dear JUMSAites

The Jos University Medical Students Association (JUMSA) Mentor-Mentee Initiative Forum is a transformative program designed to pair medical students with experienced senior mentors. This initiative aims to bolster academic excellence and facilitate the exchange of ideas to build capacity among medical students.

Led by the visionary leadership of Matabof and the JUMSA executives, this pioneering initiative has created a platform for mentees to apply for mentorship while mentors voluntarily take on the responsibility of guiding and supporting their mentees. The program seeks to foster a culture of mentorship, empowering students to realize their full potential and achieve academic success.
The forum's core objective is to enhance academic excellence by facilitating one-on-one mentorship relationships. Mentees will have the opportunity to engage with experienced mentors who have demonstrated exceptional academic achievements, professional accomplishments, and a genuine commitment to student success.
Through a structured program, mentees will receive personalized guidance, academic support, and career counseling. These mentorship relationships will provide mentees with valuable insights, study techniques, time management skills, and other strategies for academic success. Furthermore, mentors will assist mentees in setting and achieving their academic goals and navigating the challenges that arise during their medical school journey.
The Mentor-Mentee Initiative Forum goes beyond a mere academic focus, acknowledging the importance of personal and professional development. Mentees will benefit from the wisdom, experience, and networking opportunities that come with connecting with senior mentors. Peer-to-peer learning, workshops, and seminars will also be organized to foster a sense of community and enable the exchange of ideas among participants.

Signed, Matabof Victor President, Jos University Medical Student Association.
